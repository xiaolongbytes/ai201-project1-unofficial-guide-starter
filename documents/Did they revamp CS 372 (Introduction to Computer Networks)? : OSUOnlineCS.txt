Labs/assignments haven't been too difficult, though.
Revamp or not, it is still a pretty heavy class and I would recommend pairing it with a light course.

[In reply to: "Did they revamp CS 372 (Introduction to Computer Networks)?"]

I don't usually jump in on these kinds of things but I hate this class. I don't mind readings, but the weekly exercises require you to understand math that isn't really taught, and while you can logic your way through a lot of it, it's painful. The book isn't bad, just dense, and the reading takes quite some time to get through. I'm in the middle of the second programming assignment now and it's unintelligible. The annoying part is, I feel like I do understand the concepts we're supposed to know, but the weekly exercises and programming projects aren't checking for that knowledge because it's easier to get a pass/fail type of answer on a math problem. The labs are fine but as others mention, straight from the textbook authors and you don't need this class to do the lab.
If you do decide to take it, consider taking it alone or paired with something really, really easy. Or, get the textbook and just spend some time reading it and watching the lectures from the author on YouTube, and skip the class.

I would not base my evaluation of a class on reddit posting, honestly.  You have a biased sample size of people who are more likely to complain.
372 course material is comprehensive -- you're not going to get spoonfed everything you need to know in the lectures.  You do need to use the lectures, the textbook, and you can cite additional helpful sources as needed, while doing projects.  The weekly summary exercises are time-consuming and take a good amount of work.  The projects take even more work, because you are expected to figure it out.  The exams were the easiest part of this class.
