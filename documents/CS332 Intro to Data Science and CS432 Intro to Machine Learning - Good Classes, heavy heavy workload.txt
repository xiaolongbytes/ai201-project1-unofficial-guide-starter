[In reply to: "CS332 Intro to Data Science and CS432 Intro to Machine Learning - Good Classes, heavy heavy workload"]

I took the first quarter of 332 and was not a fan of the class and its instructional design at all. I feel like I wasted an elective and once I heard the other courses were the same format, I opted out of completing the track.
My biggest gripe was how little original content the professor contributed. Most of the explorations consisted of small sections of original content and then links to blog posts, articles, and outside resources and videos. The professor would also link heavily to her own consulting website which had some good content but was clearly already written before she was at OSU. Additionally the flow of the content and modules felt awkward at times. We’d have writing assignments in module 3 requiring us to explain data science approaches that weren’t covered until Module 7. After a certain point I really found myself asking, what exactly was I paying for to have this class administered by OSU?
The writing assignments were a huge frustration for me as well. For an intro DS class, I spent farrrrr more time writing than I spent doing coding assignments and that was extremely frustrating. The writing assignments were also extremely repetitive and the requirements were often unclear and kinda nitpicky. The coding assignments she supplied were also very repetitive. We used the same “Titanic” data set for the entire 10-week course and after a while the learning value of it grew stale because you pretty much knew all there was to know about the data. By the end of the course I had a 50+ page report which took the majority of my time (some classmates had 100+ pg reports). Maybe my expectations were misaligned but with only 3 electives, i would have not taken 332 if I knew I would be spending so much time writing.
All that said, the professor is very knowledgeable and very kind. I just left wishing we got more of her insights, opinions, and thought leadership on data science. Instead it kinda felt the class existed because like she came across a really good remote gig and thought she could throw something simple together. For that reason I think there are much better organized and cheaper DS/ML courses out there for someone who is really interested in the topic. I do not recommend

[In reply to: "CS332 Intro to Data Science and CS432 Intro to Machine Learning - Good Classes, heavy heavy workload"]

I enjoyed all the classes so far.
Yes, 332 was a lot of documenting/writing but I understood the why. I believe the Professor was trying to get students to understand the “why” behind data science, because it’s not just about cleaning data and running models. The hard part is telling the story, choosing the best model and interpreting the results, then how to improve. People in this field typically have doctorate level degrees which means years of studying, researching and understanding.
ML was much lighter and DL has long explorations but that makes sense because the subject is more complex.
I appreciated Dr. Gates’s way of presenting the information. To me, it gave a great overview and examples/break down topics that can be extremely complex that takes people years to study and understand. She may link her site, but it’s because her site has all the same information she may use to teach the class- the concepts don’t change. I also think she’s extremely open to how people learn and how different everyone’s background is with DS so the class is meant to be available for beginners and advanced students. I also feel like she’s one of the few professors in this program who is extremely engaged in creating the material and helping students during term.
My conclusion is if you’re really interested in data science, then the class is a great introduction. If you’re already advanced then you can tailor projects to your level of knowledge and comfort but maybe you won’t find as much value. I wanted to understand what exactly is data science and how ML and DL works, so spending more time to understand the why has been enjoyable.
I spoke to some machine learning engineers and they said that coding is maybe 30% of the work. But I was able to speak to them in technical terms and understand what they were talking about which means I’ve learned a lot and I’m still taking DL this term.
