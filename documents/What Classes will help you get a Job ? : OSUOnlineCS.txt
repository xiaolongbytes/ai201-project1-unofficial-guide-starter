[In reply to: "What Classes will help you get a Job ?"]

The only classes I took that were slightly relevant to work are:
Projects (basically make a personal project and record weekly updates for the instructor for accountability)
Capstone (basically a preview of what hackathons are like, which sometimes happens at work)
Databases (SQL is eternal)
Fundamentals of Programming Languages (basically learn how to learn new programming languages)
Academic classwork is not representative of real work. Most of my time at work involves adding new features/bugfixes to huge existing codebases, helping other people troubleshoot their problems, meetings, code review, performance management, corporate volunteering, compliance coursework. There wasn't a single course at OSU that really covered any of those things. There will never be a situation where you just build an app by yourself that has a very strict list of requirements, like you will in almost every single OSU class. In actual jobs you need to work with downstream customers of your code to give them what they want, and also hassle the people upstream of you, to give you the support that you need to do your job.
Employers do not care about your classwork. They know that its not really relevant to what daily work is really like. Employers just want to make sure that you can learn new things easily, and can troubleshoot problems by yourself. For context, I did Devops for one year, and now I'm doing Data Engineer.
